# Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines (SQEG) - Part 2: E-E-A-T & YMYL Framework

This reference explains the E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) criteria and YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) standards according to Section 3.0 to 5.0 of Google's SQEG.

---

## 1. YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) Topics
Some topics are classified as YMYL because low-quality or inaccurate advice could negatively impact a person's health, financial stability, physical safety, or the welfare of society.

| YMYL Category | Core Scope / Topics Covered | Standard Applied |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Medical & Health** | Advice on physical/mental health, medical conditions, hospitals, drugs, clinical trials. | Must align with high-grade peer-reviewed medical consensus. |
| **Financial Security** | Investments, tax advice, banking, real estate, insurance, student loans. | Must be accurate, complete, and require certified/professional authority. |
| **Civic & Law** | Voting, administrative procedures, court systems, international laws, immigration. | Must come from official government portals or certified legal practitioners. |
| **Society & Identity** | Critical sociological issues, demographics, protection of minorities/groups. | Must be balanced, objective, and completely free of systemic bias. |
| **Other High-Risk** | Purchase of major assets (cars, homes), career changes, university choices. | Requires high transparency and trustworthy facts. |

## 2. The Four Pillars of E-E-A-T

### A. Experience (E)
Experience evaluates whether the creator has first-hand or life experience with the topic.
* **Everyday Expertise:** For many topics, people share personal reviews, travel guides, or recovery stories. If someone writes a review of a software they have used for 2 years, this is high Experience.
* **Lack of Experience:** A review of a phone where the writer only copies specs from the official site has low Experience.

### B. Expertise (E)
Expertise evaluates whether the creator has formal expertise, knowledge, credentials, or certifications.
* **Formal Expertise:** Critical for YMYL topics. An article about heart disease must be written by a cardiologist or medical editor. An article about plumbing codes must be written by a certified plumber.
* **Informal/Everyday Expertise:** Relevant for non-YMYL topics. Someone writing a guide to beating a video game doesn't need a degree in game design.

### C. Authoritativeness (A)
Authoritativeness measures whether the creator or website is recognized as a main source of accurate information in their field.
* **Signals:** Citations in media, awards, backlink authority, mentions by industry experts, or the website being an official source (e.g., the official Apple support page for macOS).

### D. Trustworthiness (T) - The Central Pivot
Trustworthiness is the most important pillar. It holds the other three pillars together. A page can have experience and expertise, but if it is dishonest or has a conflict of interest, it is not trustworthy.
* **Transparency:** Clear "About Me" pages, contact details, physical address, and publishing dates.
* **Neutrality:** Objective writing that does not hide drawbacks to push affiliate products.
* **Safety:** Safe payment gateways, SSL certificates, clearly defined terms of service, and return policies.
