# Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines (SQEG) - Section 3.0: E-E-A-T & Quality Ratings

This reference manual provides an exhaustive, multi-chapter deconstruction of how to evaluate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness (E-E-A-T) as defined in Sections 3.0, 4.0, and 5.0 of Google's Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines (SQEG).

---

## 1. The Core Architecture of E-E-A-T

E-E-A-T is not a single ranking factor; it is a conceptual framework Google raters use to evaluate content quality.

```
       ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
       │                       TRUST (T)                         │
       │   (The most critical pillar. Experience, Expertise,     │
       │   and Authoritativeness support this center pivot)     │
       └────────────────────────────┬────────────────────────────┘
                                    │
         ┌──────────────────────────┼──────────────────────────┐
         ▼                          ▼                          ▼
  ┌──────────────┐           ┌──────────────┐           ┌──────────────┐
  │  EXPERIENCE  │           │  EXPERTISE   │           │AUTHORITATIVE │
  │     (E)      │           │     (E)      │           │     (A)      │
  └──────────────┘           └──────────────┘           └──────────────┘
```

### A. Experience (First-Hand Knowledge)
Experience assesses whether the creator of the main content has real-life, physical, or practical experience with the topic.
* **Key indicator:** Does the content creator show they have actually performed the action they write about? E.g., showing original photos of themselves at a hotel, detailing their personal struggle with a disease, showing a video of their hands on the product.
* **Everyday Experience:** Raters are instructed to look for personal reviews, community discussions, or travel blogs. If a person writes a review of a restaurant they've frequented for five years, their experience is high.
* **Low Experience Penalty:** Pages that rewrite specifications or summarize other reviews without showing any personal contact with the product/topic are rated Low.

### B. Expertise (Formal Knowledge & Skill)
Expertise assesses whether the content creator has the formal credentials, education, licensing, or professional experience required for the topic.
* **Key indicator:** Medical degrees (MD, PhD), legal licensing (Bar qualifications), financial certification (CPA, CFA), or technical training.
* **Formal vs. Everyday Expertise:**
  * For YMYL topics (like medical diagnosis, tax filing, structural engineering), formal expertise is mandatory.
  * For non-YMYL topics (like gardening, video games, home decoration), informal or everyday expertise is sufficient. A highly skilled home gardener can write a "High Page Quality" guide without a botanical degree.

### C. Authoritativeness (Industry Recognition)
Authoritativeness measures whether the creator or website is widely accepted as a primary source of accurate information in their field.
* **Key indicator:** Are they cited, referenced, or recommended by other experts?
* **Signals of Authority:**
  * Infiltration in Wikipedia (the author or site has dedicated Wikipedia coverage indicating notable status).
  * Backlinks from high-authority institutional websites (e.g., medical advice linked by WHO, legal advice cited by government courts).
  * Industry awards and recognitions.

### D. Trustworthiness (The Core Pillar)
Trustworthiness is the central pivot of E-E-A-T. A page can show experience and expertise, but if it is deceptive, biased, or lacks transparency, it is not trustworthy.
* **Pillars of Trust:**
  * **Transparency:** Who wrote the content and who publishes the website? YMYL sites require full editorial profiles, real author names, bios, contact forms, phone numbers, and physical addresses.
  * **Factual Accuracy:** Does the content align with consensus? For YMYL health advice, does it reflect medical fact or pseudo-science?
  * **Honesty & Disclosures:** Clearly visible labeling of sponsored posts, conflict of interest declarations, and affiliate disclosures. A review that hides flaws to push affiliate sales is rated Low.
  * **Security:** Clear transaction policies, SSL certification, refund policies, and data privacy safeguards.

## 2. Topic-Specific E-E-A-T Requirements Matrix

| Topic Classification | Type of E-E-A-T Required | Core Assessment Criteria |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High-Stake YMYL Medical** | Professional, certified expert. | Articles must be written by doctors/medical experts, referenced with medical journals, and checked by an editorial board. |
| **High-Stake YMYL Financial** | Accredited financial advisors, CPAs. | Must use current tax/financial law, cover risks, and exclude unrealistic get-rich-quick claims. |
| **High-Stake YMYL Legal** | Certified attorneys, government portals. | Must reference updated statutes, court codes, and state jurisdictions. |
| **Hobbies / Crafts** | Everyday experience and passion. | Evaluate the detail of the guide, original photos, helpful tips, and physical proof. |
| **Product Reviews** | First-hand experience and testing. | Raters look for original pictures, testing videos, pros and cons, and comparisons to competitors. |
| **News / Current Events** | Editorial oversight and professional journalism. | Must follow standard ethics, name sources, fact-check stories, and update corrections. |
