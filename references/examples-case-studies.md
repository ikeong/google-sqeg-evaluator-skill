# Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines (SQEG) - Part 5: Quality Rating Practice & Examples

This reference summarizes real-world examples and rating case studies provided in the SQEG to guide human raters on PQ and NM decisions.

---

## 1. Page Quality (PQ) Rating Examples

### A. Highest PQ Example Case
* **Topic:** Chronic Kidney Disease Symptoms (YMYL).
* **Website:** Mayo Clinic.
* **Analysis:** Mayo Clinic is a world-renowned medical institution. The article is written and reviewed by professional medical doctors, based on clinical trials and established scientific consensus. 
* **E-E-A-T:** High Expertise (MD credentials), High Authority (highly cited), High Trust (transparent contact details and editorial policies).
* **Rating:** **Highest**

### B. High PQ Example Case
* **Topic:** How to clean a DSL camera sensor (Non-YMYL).
* **Creator:** A professional nature photographer.
* **Content:** Detailed step-by-step description with close-up photos of the actual sensor showing how to handle dust spots. Includes warnings about scratch risks.
* **E-E-A-T:** High Experience (first-hand photos and warnings). No formal university degree is needed since this is a hobby/craft.
* **Rating:** **High**

### C. Low PQ Example Case
* **Topic:** Symptoms of heart attack (YMYL).
* **Website:** A general lifestyle blog.
* **Content:** Short article with a list of generic symptoms. Author is listed as a guest writer with no bio or health credentials. No medical consensus reference or doctor review is mentioned.
* **E-E-A-T:** Low Expertise, Low Trust. The topic is high-risk YMYL but lacks professional authority.
* **Rating:** **Low**

### D. Lowest PQ Example Case
* **Topic:** Credit card refund trick (YMYL).
* **Website:** An anonymous financial tips blog.
* **Content:** Describes a method to get credit card refunds by exploiting bank code errors. This is actually a guide on how to commit wire fraud.
* **Reputation:** The page encourages illegal actions.
* **Rating:** **Lowest**

## 2. Needs Met (NM) Rating Examples

### A. Fully Meets (FullyM) Example Case
* **Query:** `Amazon.com customer login`
* **Result:** `https://www.amazon.com/gp/navigation/redirector.html/signin`
* **Explanation:** This is a clear Website Query. The user wants the official login page. The result takes them directly to the sign-in screen.
* **Rating:** **Fully Meets**

### B. Highly Meets (HM) Example Case
* **Query:** `How many states in USA`
* **Result:** A page listing all 50 states, detailing when they joined the union, with an interactive US map.
* **Explanation:** A Know query. The page completely answers the question and adds valuable context.
* **Rating:** **Highly Meets**

### C. Moderately Meets (MM) Example Case
* **Query:** `Shamsi calendar conversion`
* **Result:** A general history of the Solar Hijri calendar with a small manual conversion formula at the bottom of the page.
* **Explanation:** The user wanted tool utility (conversion), but received a history lesson with a manual formula instead. The page has value, but is not fully satisfying.
* **Rating:** **Moderately Meets**

### D. Fails to Meet (FailsM) Example Case
* **Query:** `Best running shoes for flat feet`
* **Result:** A directory website with expired domains or empty pages showing placeholder ads.
* **Rating:** **Fails to Meet**
