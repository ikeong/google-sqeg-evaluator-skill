# Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines (SQEG) - Part 1: Page Quality (PQ) Guidelines

This reference covers the specific rules and methodologies for determining Page Quality (PQ) according to Sections 1.0 through 9.0 of Google's SQEG.

---

## 1. The Concept of Page Quality (PQ)
The goal of PQ rating is to evaluate how well the page achieves its **beneficial purpose**. A page can have a wide range of helpful purposes including:
* Sharing information about a topic.
* Entertaining (humor, art, music).
* Expressing personal opinions, stories, and creativity.
* Sharing files, images, videos, or music.
* Selling products or services.
* Allowing users to ask and answer questions.
* Online gaming, interactive calculators, or utilities.

If a page has no beneficial purpose, or if it is designed to deceive, spread hate, incite violence, or cause physical/financial harm, it must be rated **Lowest**.

---

## 2. Page Components: MC, SC, and Ads
To rate a page, we must distinguish between three parts:

### A. Main Content (MC)
MC is any part of the page that directly helps the page achieve its purpose. MC includes:
* The body text of an article.
* The main video or image on a page.
* The primary tool, game, widget, or form.
* User-generated content (UGC) like comments, reviews, or forum posts *if* they are the primary focus of the page.

### B. Supplementary Content (SC)
SC is content that contributes to a good user experience but does not directly help the page achieve its primary purpose. SC:
* Helps users navigate to other sections (menus, search bars, category pages).
* Showcases related articles or products.
* Enhances the visual appeal.
* Must not distract from or overlap with the MC.

### C. Advertisements & Monetization (Ads)
Ads are elements placed to monetize the page.
* Ads must be clearly marked and separated from MC and SC.
* The presence of ads alone is not a sign of low quality.
* The critical factor is how intrusive they are. If ads overlap, push MC below the fold unnecessarily, or simulate MC to trick users into clicking, they cause a **Low** or **Lowest** rating.

---

## 3. The Quality Rater Questionnaire for PQ Assessment
When assessing a page, a rater must answer three core questions:
1. What is the beneficial purpose of the page? How well does it serve that purpose?
2. What is the E-E-A-T rating for the page?
3. What is the quality and quantity of the Main Content? (Does it show adequate effort, talent, or skill?)

---

## 4. Rating Main Content (MC) Quality
Raters assess the quality of MC based on the effort, originality, talent, and skill required to produce it.

### A. Elements of High-Quality MC:
* **Effort:** Deep research, detailed explanations, custom illustrations, or hours of work.
* **Originality:** Original reporting, unique calculations, proprietary testing of products, or interviewing experts.
* **Talent/Skill:** Highly professional layout, clear formatting, and expert editorial polish.

### B. Elements of Low-Quality MC:
* **Thin Content:** Lack of details, brief summaries of other pages, or superficial definitions.
* **Auto-Generated Content (Scraped/Translated/Low-Quality AI):** Content generated automatically with no human editing, review, or factual checks.
* **Clickbait/Sensationalist Titles:** Exaggerated, shocking, or misleading headlines which do not match the content.
