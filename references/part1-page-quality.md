# Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines (SQEG) - Section 1: Page Quality (PQ) General Overview & topology

This reference manual reproduces, translates, and formats the concepts of Part 1 (Page Quality rating guidelines) of Google's Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines (SQEG) in exhaustive depth.

---

## 1. Introduction to Page Quality Rating (Section 1.0)
A Page Quality (PQ) rating is a score that reflects how well a page achieves its beneficial purpose using Google's standards. Raters are instructed to look at the page and ask:
* Does this page have a beneficial purpose?
* Does it help users, or is it created to make money with no care for the user's experience?
* Is there any attempt to deceive, scam, or harm searchers?

---

## 2. Page Classification & Topology (Section 2.0)
A webpage is analyzed as a system composed of three core parts:
1. **Main Content (MC):**
   * The actual text, images, videos, tools, calculators, forms, games, or interactive applications that directly fulfill the beneficial purpose of the page.
   * Raters are instructed to verify the origin, quality, and creation effort of the MC.
2. **Supplementary Content (SC):**
   * Sidebars, navigation links, related article suggestions, header menus, footer links, and user interface features.
   * SC is evaluated on whether it contributes to a helpful User Experience (UX) or diverts attention from the MC.
3. **Advertisements / Monetization (Ads):**
   * Banner ads, affiliate links, sponsored posts, paywalls, and newsletters.
   * Google does not penalize pages for having ads. However, if the ads are placed of in a way that blocks or distracts from the MC, the page is rated lower.

---

## 3. Webpage Accountability Criteria (Section 2.5)
A high-quality website must be completely transparent about who is responsible for its content. The level of required transparency depends on the risk of the topic:
* **Non-YMYL Pages:** Simple personal blogs only need a nickname or username, and an email.
* **Store / Transactional Pages:** Must display physical business addresses, official company registrations, tax IDs, refund/return policies, telephone support, and email support.
* **YMYL Portals:** Must outline their editorial standards, peer review processes, board certifications, and list the names of their authors/editors with links to their bios.
